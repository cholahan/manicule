3. Linking back to the novels

In these cases where the 'sentiments' are summaries of passages or observations on entire scenes, Richardson is identifying the moral lesson that he wants his readers to take away from the novels. Take, for example, the maxim:

Wicked men will stoop to the most degrading meanness to obtain a favourite end”

This maxim is taken from page 270 of the first volume of Pamela, from a scene in which Mr. B dresses up as a female servant and pretends to fall asleep in Pamela’s closet while she is undressing. By offering these summaries, Richardson uses his Moral Sentiments as a way to interpret the novel for his readers: to control, in other words, how they construe, and learn from, the fictional scenes they read.
