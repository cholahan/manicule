2. Organizational Structure

In one of the letters that Richardson attaches at the end of the work, he clarifies that his novels, while touching on similar broad topics—-in each novel section, for example, we get sections on “Love” and “Beauty”-—were written to address different issues. Richardson claims that he does not continue his novel Grandison to include section on parenting because he has already addressed this in his earlier Pamela:

“As to what you are pleased to hint of the Hero’s appearing to shine in the parental character, have I not in PAMELA entered into that subject pretty largely? And have I not in this history avoided touching upon the same topics that I have treated on in either of the two former?” (406).
