2. Organizational Structure

The division of the ‘sentiments’ by novel suggest that Richardson believed he could not fully separate the moral lessons from their contexts: from the characters and plots of the novel. It is possible that he also imagined the readers of Moral Sentiments to turn to specific novels for specific advice. Many of the topics tend to reflect the novels from which they are ‘extracted’. For example, the section on “Duty” from Clarissa here discusses, for the most part, the duty of children to their parents. The section on "Duty" from Pamela, in contrast, discusses a servant’s duty to their superiors (or, at times, lack thereof). This suggests that Richardson imagined that readers of Moral Sentiments were already intimately familiar with his novels.
