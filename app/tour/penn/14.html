2. Organizational Structure

Playing with format

Richardson also strategically uses the book’s organizational structure to forefront certain moral lessons and guide the reader to certains sentiments. The organizational structure itself, then, becomes a medium through which to interpret and comment upon the original novels.

The section on Pamela, for example, begins with “Address to the Rich,” seperate from the section on “The Rich, Riches.” We can assume, then, that Richardson named the heading as “Address to the Rich” so that it would be found at the beginning of the section “A,” as a way to forefront these sentiments at the beginning of the section. It is likely that he is using the alphabetical scheme, then, to immediately offer the reader what he sees as the most important moral lesson of Pamela: it's commentary on upper class society.
