2. Organizational Structure

Approximately 75% of the subjects in Grandison and Pamela cross-reference another section (107 out of the total 142 headings in Grandison and 92 out of the 122 sections in Pamela). In Clarissa, the first novel that Richardson ‘commonplaced’, only 26% of the headings cross-reference another topic. In addition to the fact that the Clarissa section has fewer cross-references overall (275 total, compared to the 372 in Pamela and 674 in Grandison), they seem to be clustered under certain subjects.

Given that Richardson reproduced much of the Clarissa section from the 1751 appendix version, it is likely that the cross-references were added afterwards, and only to a select few subject sections, while the cross-references for Pamela and Grandison were produced during the compilation process. This suggests that as Richardson was creating the collection of Moral Sentiments, the cross-references and networked nature of the work became more critical to his project.
