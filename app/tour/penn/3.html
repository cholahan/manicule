1. Introduction

Richardson published A Collection of Moral Sentiments in the March of 1755. The book contains maxims, aphorisms, advice, and sayings from Richardson’s three novels: Pamela, Clarissa, and Sir Charles Grandison. The ‘sentiments’ found in these pages are quotations from the original novels, pithy statements that summarize a moral or lesson of the novel, or references to passages in the original book.
