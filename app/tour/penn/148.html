3. Linking back to the novels

We find a similar case here, in the section on Heath in Clarissa. Richardson quotes Joseph Addison - though he employs Addison's words on liberty to comment, instead, on health:

“What Mr. Addison says of Liberty, may, with still greater propriety, be said of Health; for what is Liberty itself without Health?
It makes the gloomy face of Nature gay;
Gives beauty to the Sun, and pleasure to the Day.”




The following two lines reprinted here are also credited to Addison in the original passage from Clarissa on page 30 of volume six. The lines are taken from Addison's poem “To the Right Honourable the Lord Halifax, an earlier draft of Addison's A Letter from Italy (1704). The original lines read:

“O Liberty— [...]
Thou mak'st the gloomy face of Nature gay,
Giv'st beauty to the sun, & pleasure to the day”
